## 0. What this doc inherits, and what it does not touch

Before anything else, the commitments that do not get renegotiated here:

- The product is **Plan**, a single-user three-column Kanban today (v0.1 is shipped on `main`), and a Kanban + **Multi-Agent Research Council** from v0.4 onward.
- The Council is **three agents** (Researcher, Consolidator, Critic) that speak to the user as **one voice**. This is a locked vision decision (Q3, Q6 in [phase-02-vision-progress.md](../tracking/phase-02-vision-progress.md)). Marketing does not reveal three agents as three separate personas to the reader; it can explain the architecture, but the voice stays singular.
- The **Write Gate** is a locked product principle: the Council reads the board and proposes changes, but it never writes without an explicit user tap (Q4). Marketing claims must honour this. No "AI that runs your board for you" language, ever.
- The **editorial-quiet, warm, first-person, no-emoji** tone is locked from v0.1.0 and re-confirmed for v0.4 (Q3). All outbound copy inherits this.
- **Persistence** is Supabase Postgres behind a repository boundary (Q7). Marketing claims about memory need to stay accurate: the Consolidator remembers *modes used, session summaries, emotional/individual context, and the user's spoken wisdom*. It does NOT scrape the entire board, fine-tune, or read third-party data.
- **Releases ship serially** (v0.4 → v0.5 → v0.6 → v1.0) per [operating-model.md](../operating-model.md). This plan assumes that ordering.

Out of scope for this document:

- Phase 06 (Brand Identity). No logo, no colour system, no typography pass. That phase gets its own methodology.
- Product PRD claims. If this document and a PRD disagree, the PRD wins; flag it in section 9 (revision log) and open a separate PR.
- Any change to the v0.1 shipped tone. If v0.4's outbound voice needs to evolve, that evolution is captured here as a proposal, not as a unilateral change.

How to use this doc:

- Any section flagged `[CD TO CONFIRM]` is a judgment call that depends on CD's relationships, comfort, or budget — not something a planning agent should decide alone.
- This is a living document. Edit in place; log material changes in section 9.

## 1. Positioning

### 1.1 One-line pitch (proposed)

> **A small Kanban with a quiet thinking partner. It reads your board, raises honest objections, and proposes changes you approve with a tap.**

`[CD TO CONFIRM]` — this is a draft. Two alternative framings to consider:

- Council-first: *"Three minds, one voice, one board. Plan's Research Council thinks with you about the week ahead."*
- Kanban-first: *"The simplest Kanban that ever asked you a good question."*

The proposed line leads with the Kanban (known category) and introduces the Council as an adjective of the product, not the product itself. This is deliberate: the Kanban is the wedge; the Council is the reason to stay.

### 1.2 Three-line description (proposed)

> Plan is a three-column Kanban board for one person thinking through their own work.
> It ships with a small Research Council — three specialized minds that speak to you as one quiet voice — to help you decompose a goal, review a stale board, or think out loud on a Monday morning.
> The Council reads and proposes; every change waits for your tap.

`[CD TO CONFIRM]` — especially the third sentence. The Write Gate is the most important claim in the product, so it belongs in the description. If CD prefers to lead with the Kanban's local-first heritage instead, the third line could become: *"Your data stays yours; your board stays yours; the Council only proposes."*

### 1.3 Who Plan is NOT for

Plan is not for:

- **Teams that need real-time multi-user collaboration today.** v0.5 adds small-team support with Supabase auth, but Plan is not and will never be Jira. If a team needs swim-lanes, sprints, story points, or admin dashboards, they should buy a team tool.
- **Buyers shopping for plugins and integrations.** Plan has no Slack bot, no GitHub sync, no Zapier. The opinionated Kanban is a feature, not a roadmap gap.
- **Anyone who wants an AI that acts without asking.** The Write Gate means every board change is a proposal the user taps. If someone wants an agent that drafts emails, runs code, or applies changes autonomously, Plan is not it.
- **Customizers.** Three fixed columns. One card shape. v0.6 adds multi-list, a Tracker view, and task branching, but the Kanban stays opinionated. If the buying question is "can I rename the columns," the answer is no, and that is a permanent answer.
- **AI-skeptics allergic to any in-product thinking surface.** The Council's thinking-stream aesthetic is central; a reader who wants the AI completely off the screen will find Plan uncomfortable.

## 2. Audience segmentation

Three personas, in priority order for the v0.4 → v1.0 run-up. Persona A is the alpha target. Persona B is the public-beta target. Persona C is the v0.5 teams-beta target.

### 2.1 Persona A — Indie developer (v0.4 alpha primary)

**Who:** A one-to-three-year indie builder, shipping side projects alongside a day job or a small SaaS solo. Writes TypeScript, Go, Rust, or Swift. Deploys to Vercel, Fly.io, or a VPS. Reads commit messages carefully. Keeps a running `ideas.md`.

**Where they hang out online:**

- Hacker News, Lobste.rs
- Mastodon dev circles, X (developer Twitter)
- Indie Hackers forums
- `/r/SideProject`, `/r/selfhosted`, `/r/devtools`
- Discord communities around specific frameworks (Next.js, SvelteKit, T3, etc.) — `[CD TO CONFIRM]` which ones CD has genuine presence in; presence beats membership

**What they read:**

- Fly.io blog, Vercel blog (for the deep posts, not the announcements)
- Julia Evans, Drew DeVault, Dan Abramov, Simon Willison
- The Pragmatic Engineer (free tier), Bytes, JavaScript Weekly, TLDR DevOps
- Pieter Levels on X; Every.to for its engineering-culture essays
- Dev YouTube: Theo Browne, Fireship, The Primeagen (clips, not live streams)

**Pain they'd pay to reduce:**

The Monday-morning side-project cold start. They have 90 minutes before the day job begins. They open the editor, then close it, because they cannot remember what the next concrete step was. The morning-greeting experience in Plan's Council (full-screen card, calm prompt, board read back to them) is built for exactly this moment. The pitch to them is not "AI for your todos"; it is "a Kanban that asks you where you left off."

### 2.2 Persona B — Solo knowledge worker (v0.6 public-beta primary)

**Who:** A writer, researcher, consultant, or solopreneur whose core output is prose and ideas, not code. Probably uses Notion, Obsidian, Craft, or a mix, and is quietly dissatisfied with each. Runs a one-person business or a long creative project (book, research thread, launch).

**Where they hang out online:**

- Substack, both as reader and writer
- X, Threads, Bluesky (varies by sub-persona)
- `/r/productivity`, `/r/ObsidianMD`, `/r/Notion`
- The Notion community forums; Craft / Bear enthusiast spaces
- Small, paid-community Slacks around specific thinkers (Forte Labs, Ness Labs, etc.) — `[CD TO CONFIRM]` access

**What they read:**

- Ness Labs, Every, Stratechery, Not Boring
- Newsletters from Tiago Forte (Building a Second Brain), Anne-Laure Le Cunff (Maker Mind), Cal Newport
- Podcasts: Deep Questions (Newport), Focused (Sparks + Kaufman), Cortex (Grey + Snell)
- Books: *Deep Work*, *Ultralearning*, *Building a Second Brain*, *Getting Things Done* (read once, discarded, still cited)

**Pain they'd pay to reduce:**

The gap between a fuzzy goal ("write the chapter this month") and a week's worth of concrete actions. Notion goes too loose; Asana goes too rigid; paper gets lost. A Council that can sit with a paragraph of intent and ask four good questions before proposing a breakdown is a real wedge — provided it does not feel like a chatbot. The no-emoji, editorial voice is explicitly built for this reader.

### 2.3 Persona C — Small-team lead (v0.5 teams-beta primary)

**Who:** A first-time or second-time lead of a three-to-eight-person team. Engineering manager at an early-stage startup, a two-person ops pair, a founding design trio. The person in the team who is "the glue" — repeating context, chasing blockers, writing the weekly status note everyone else would rather skip.

**Where they hang out online:**

- Rands Leadership Slack, Engineering Leadership Community (ELC) Slack — `[CD TO CONFIRM]` which of these CD is actually a member of; we should not market in a community we do not live in
- `/r/engineeringmanagers`
- LinkedIn (for the senior sub-segment; the junior sub-segment treats LinkedIn as a career surface only)
- Lenny's Subreddit; Lenny's community

**What they read:**

- Lenny's Newsletter, The Pragmatic Engineer
- Will Larson (*Elegant Puzzle*, *An Elegant Puzzle*, Irrational Exuberance)
- Camille Fournier (*The Manager's Path*, Cutlefish)
- Charity Majors (honeycomb blog + Twitter)
- Podcasts: Lenny's Podcast, Engineering Enablement, The Pragmatic Engineer Podcast

**Pain they'd pay to reduce:**

Being the single point of continuity on the team. The Council in v0.5 (post-auth, post-multi-user) can read a shared board and surface the things nobody else is tracking — stale cards, implicit decisions, the proposal nobody has approved yet. The pitch to this persona is never "replace your 1:1s"; it is "be the second brain for your team's plan, so you can stop being it yourself."

## 3. Channel plan per release

One principle across all four: **own the slow channels; borrow the fast ones**. Owned newsletters, demo videos, and case studies accrue to us over time. HN, Product Hunt, and podcasts are borrowed attention that we stage carefully and do not lean on every week.

### 3.1 v0.4 (alpha) — "build in public, small ring of believers"

**Posture:** We are proving the Council does not feel creepy to real users. Feedback loop, not launch. A private alpha ring (5–15 users, hand-invited).

**Channels (2–3):**

1. **Developer dev-log on X + Mastodon.** Build-in-public posts, 2–3 per week. Screenshots of the Council reveal. Sparingly linked to demo videos.
2. **Owned long-form channel** (personal Substack or equivalent — `[CD TO CONFIRM]` whether CD already has one or wants to spin one up). One essay every two weeks on a specific Council behaviour. These are the assets that survive the release cycle.
3. **Private alpha ring.** Hand-invited, DM/email-only. Weekly one-question survey. This is not really a public channel; it is the feedback engine that produces the material for channels 1 and 2.

**Deliberately NOT:** Show HN, Product Hunt, podcast tour. We save those for v0.6.

**Cadence:** Three build notes per week; one essay every two weeks; one 60–90s demo video per Council capability as it ships.

**Signal metric per channel:**

- **Dev log:** substantive replies that describe a specific Council behaviour (for example, "the Critic caught X in my plan"). Not likes, not reposts. A single thoughtful reply per post is the bar in month one.
- **Newsletter:** reply rate, not open rate. A reader who replies saw themselves in the post.
- **Alpha ring:** **week-2 Council-session count per user.** If they come back the second week, they will come back the third. This is the highest-signal metric in the whole alpha.

### 3.2 v0.5 (teams beta) — "earning trust in leadership circles"

**Posture:** The Council is no longer just a solo tool. We enter small-team-lead communities with case studies from alpha users, not cold pitches.

**Channels (2–3):**

1. **Existing alpha-user case studies.** Published on our owned channel (the Substack from v0.4). Each case study names the person (with permission) and shows a real Council session. These are the single highest-leverage content we produce.
2. **Engineering-leadership newsletter guest essays or short cross-posts.** Unpaid, earned via warm intros. `[CD TO CONFIRM]` which newsletters are within reach — Lenny's, TPE, and Will Larson's *Irrational Exuberance* are the obvious asks, but we should not pretend we have relationships we do not have.
3. **Leadership Slack communities.** Rands Leadership, ELC, plus any the CD is already in. Answer questions about planning, share the Council's thinking-stream reveal only when genuinely relevant to the thread. Zero pitches. `[CD TO CONFIRM]` whether CD is comfortable being present under his real name in these spaces; if not, skip this channel rather than ghost-write.

**Cadence:** One case study per month; one guest essay per month (stretch); weekly real-answer participation in Slack communities.

**Signal metric per channel:**

- **Case studies:** inbound DMs asking for a v0.5 beta invite. Not page views.
- **Guest essays:** sign-up conversions via a dedicated referral link. Not "impressions."
- **Slack communities:** warm intros to candidate beta teams. A measurable form of "did we earn the right to be here."

### 3.3 v0.6 (public beta) — "the opening, staged carefully"

**Posture:** The launch ramp. We are opening the gate, but paid spend is still zero.

**Channels (2–3):**

1. **Show HN + Product Hunt, timed together.** Timed to the Tracker-view + task-branching milestone. We do this once, carefully. `[CD TO CONFIRM]` timing: Tuesday or Wednesday of a quiet week, avoid major conference weeks. No paid hunter on Product Hunt; run it in CD's name.
2. **Short-form video.** Loom + YouTube Shorts + X clips demoing task-branching and Tracker view. Two to three per week across the six-week launch window.
3. **Targeted newsletter cross-posts.** Re-use v0.5 relationships; do not start new ones from scratch for the public-beta launch.

**Cadence:** Six-week launch window. One major owned post per capability milestone; daily engagement on comments.

**Signal metric per channel:**

- **HN:** quality of the discussion, not points. Look for replies from identifiable developers who describe their own planning workflow, not "cool product."
- **Product Hunt:** day-30 retention of the launch-day sign-up cohort. Not day-1 ranking.
- **Video:** activation rate — percentage of viewers who sign up and create a first board within 48 hours. Track with a dedicated referral URL per clip.

### 3.4 v1.0 (full launch) — "confident ramp, earned + small paid"

**Posture:** The launch moment. Owned assets are polished; earned attention is lined up; a small paid budget (`[CD TO CONFIRM]` ceiling) covers one or two precision placements.

**Channels (2–3):**

1. **Owned launch moment.** One launch essay on the Substack; one hero launch video; one polished landing page. No drip campaign, no five-part email series.
2. **Earned podcast tour.** Three to five appearances on productivity and dev podcasts. `[CD TO CONFIRM]` target list; needs four to six weeks lead time. Realistic asks: Lenny's Podcast, Deep Questions, Focused, The Pragmatic Engineer Podcast, one indie-hackers-shaped show.
3. **Paid (if budget lands).** One productivity-newsletter sponsorship aimed at Persona B; one engineering-leadership sponsorship aimed at Persona C. Skip social ads entirely — retention on cold-clicked sign-ups is brutal and would mis-shape our activation metrics.

**Cadence:** Three weeks of lead-up; launch day; four weeks of follow-up content. Owned launch essay locks two weeks before launch.

**Signal metric per channel:**

- **Owned:** day-30 Council-session count from the launch cohort. Retention, not sign-ups.
- **Podcasts:** qualified sign-ups (created a board in first 48 hours) per show's referral link. NPS from the first 100 activated users.
- **Paid:** CAC-to-session ratio. If one sponsored sign-up costs more than five times a cold sign-up, the channel is killed the following month.

## 4. Content backlog (8–12 ideas tied to Council behaviour)

Every idea below starts from something the Council actually does. None of them require invented features.

1. **"The Critic saved me from a scope creep — here's the log."** Annotated Council session transcript where the Critic's objection reshaped the proposal before it hit the user. Uses the "How I got here" reveal as the core artefact. Format: essay with inline screenshots.
2. **"What my Council asked me on a Monday morning."** Full morning-greeting transcript + a short reflection on what it surfaced. Shows the full-screen opener and the collapse-to-bottom-shelf transition. Format: screenshot-heavy walkthrough.
3. **"Three minds, one voice."** Essay on the design decision to present Researcher + Consolidator + Critic as a single voice to the user. Uses the reveal-on-demand toggle as the illustrative hook. Format: essay.
4. **"The Write Gate is the whole product."** Short essay on why the Council reads and proposes but never writes without a user tap. Anchor for every "does your AI edit my board" question, for the lifetime of the product. Format: short essay.
5. **"No emoji, no bullet soup."** On why Plan's Council replies look like an editor wrote them. Side-by-side mockups: a real Plan reply vs. a hypothetical bullet-list reply from a generic assistant. Format: essay + comparison screenshot.
6. **"The morning greeting."** Sixty-to-ninety-second demo of the full-screen Council opener on app launch. Format: Loom or YouTube Short.
7. **"Bottom shelf, not right sidebar."** Design-rationale post on protecting the three-column Kanban's horizontal width. Pulls directly from the Q3 rationale. Format: essay with mockup comparisons.
8. **"Advise mode on a stale board."** Case study of the Council reviewing a board that has not been touched in two weeks. Shows proposal cards for archiving, re-prioritising, or re-asking. Format: walkthrough with anonymised board screenshots.
9. **"Thinking-stream, not chat bubbles."** UX-writing post on why Plan renders Council output as live tokens rather than a chat transcript. Explains the cold-start B-tier vs. warm-session A-tier latency calls from Q7. Format: essay + short video clip of the rendering.
10. **"How the Consolidator remembers."** Explainer on the Consolidator's scoped memory — modes used, session summaries, emotional/individual context, the user's spoken wisdom. Explicitly NOT board-scraping; NOT fine-tuning; NOT third-party data. Format: essay + simple architecture diagram.
11. **"Three modes, one Consolidator."** Side-by-side demo: Plan mode, Advise mode, Chat mode, back-to-back with the same Consolidator remembering across all three. Format: two-to-three-minute video.
12. **"Costs, honestly."** Post-mortem of a week of real Council sessions: token budgets vs. the 5k / 40k / 10k caps from Q7. Shows we are thinking carefully about cost, which matters to both the indie-dev and small-team-lead personas. Format: essay + small table.

`[CD TO CONFIRM]` — this list is a superset. CD should green-light the first four for v0.4, the middle four for v0.5, and the last four for v0.6 / v1.0 — but that sequencing is a CD judgment call.

## 5. Community / support surface

**Decision: GitHub Discussions.** One surface, not many. The repo is already public (`freshproduceuae-max/simple-kanban`), so the infrastructure cost is zero.

**Reasoning:**

- **Audience fit.** Persona A (indie developer) already lives on GitHub. Persona B (solo knowledge worker) knows what a GitHub thread looks like because Obsidian, Quartz, and most indie tools they use point there.
- **Solo-developer sustainability.** A Discord burns out one person faster than any other community surface. A question on Discord demands a reply within hours; a question on Discussions can wait until the morning. For a solo CD running the build, planning phases, *and* support, that asymmetry is the whole ball game.
- **Public + searchable.** A Discussion becomes a permanent SEO hit for the next user with the same question. A Discord answer is lost on page two within a day.
- **Zero auth onboarding.** Both primary personas already have GitHub accounts. No login wall, no invite friction.
- **Natural cross-link graph.** Issues → Discussions → feature backlog lives as one graph. A user's question can graduate to an issue; an issue can link back to the original discussion.

**Trade-off acknowledged:** Less warmth than Discord; no real-time hallway. Accepted — because the product itself is quiet. A chatty Discord would contradict the Council's whole posture. If a real-time surface becomes necessary post-v1.0 (it probably will not for a long time), revisit; do not start with both.

**Deferred:**

- Discord: v1.0-and-later consideration, gated on demonstrated demand that a second channel can serve better than the first.
- Notion-hosted board: rejected. Closed by default, not indexable, requires Notion accounts, no native GitHub graph. All downside, no upside for our persona mix.

## 6. Brand voice

Sits atop the v0.1.0 locked tone — *editorial-quiet, warm, first-person, no emoji* — and extends it for outbound marketing. In-product copy stays verbatim with the locked tone.

**Three adjectives:**

- **Editorial.** The Council writes like a magazine editor, not a chatbot. Complete sentences. One idea per paragraph. Opinions, not hedges.
- **Warm.** First-person when natural. Plain language. The reader is a peer, not a prospect.
- **Precise.** Claims land accurately. "The Council reads and proposes" — not "the Council manages your board for you." The Write Gate is not a slogan; it is a fact, and the voice treats it as one.

**Three things the voice is NOT:**

- **Not breezy.** No "Hey there!", no exclamation points at the ends of sentences, no "Let's gooo", no startup-casual.
- **Not hype-y.** No "game-changing", no "revolutionary", no "the future of planning", no landing-page superlatives.
- **Not AI-fluent.** No "As your AI assistant", no "I'm thrilled to help you", no apologising-for-the-model tics ("As a language model…"). The Council speaks as a Council, not as a model.

**Compatibility note:** This is a strict extension of the locked v0.1.0 tone, not a new direction. Phase 06 (Brand Identity) will own the typography, palette, and logo; this document only governs the *verbal* register of outbound marketing and social copy. If Phase 06 later wants to change the verbal register, that change overrides this section; flag it in section 9.

## 7. Budget reality

**v0.4 through v0.6 (approximately 12–18 months from 2026-04-20): $0 paid spend.** Every channel in sections 3.1–3.3 is owned, earned, or relationship-based. Concrete implications:

- No sponsored newsletter placements.
- No paid social ads.
- No paid PR outreach firms.
- No paid Product Hunt hunter.
- No paid launch partners.
- Design and asset production is in-house. If Phase 06 pulls Gemini in for a dedicated brand pass (per [operating-model.md](../operating-model.md)), that is the only external creative cost through v0.6.

**v1.0: small TBD paid budget.** `[CD TO CONFIRM]` ceiling. Rough three-bucket reserve, pending the ceiling:

1. **One productivity-newsletter sponsorship** aimed at Persona B. Ness Labs, Every, or a close analogue.
2. **One engineering-leadership newsletter sponsorship** aimed at Persona C. Lenny's or TPE paid tier; skip if already covered by an earned guest essay.
3. **Launch-day hero video production.** DIY if the ceiling is tight; `[CD TO CONFIRM]` cost range if shot professionally.

**Principles for every dollar spent:**

- **Skip top-of-funnel volume.** Plan is a quiet product; low-intent clicks will bounce and skew activation math. Pay only for warm audiences who already read about planning and thinking tools.
- **Measure by day-30 activated users, never sign-ups.** A sponsored placement that ships 1,000 sign-ups and 12 activated users is worse than an unpaid essay that ships 40 sign-ups and 20 activated users.
- **Kill the channel if it cannot pass activation bar in four weeks.** No "give it another month." If the math is not there by week four, it will not be there by week eight.
- **Do not run two paid placements simultaneously in launch month.** One at a time, so attribution stays honest.
